\begin{abstract}
This paper extends the Tsai-Lee framework to software transactional memory, replacing complex thread-safe architectures with modern primitives like Compare-And-Swap. By formalizing our lightweight Announce-Array protocol in TLA+, we prove the linearizability of atomic transitions under high concurrency. We demonstrate how formal specifications verify that universal algorithms maintain correctness when built from specialized components, and compare the respective specification approaches.
\end{abstract}
